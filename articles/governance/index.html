Governance


Local Level

Presently, Metro Transit is owned by the City of Madison and is a utility similar to such entities as police, fire, water, sewerage and streets. The main governing unit is the city's Transit and Parking Commission that consists of 3 Alders, 6 regular citizen members, and 2 citizen alternate members who can vote in case a regular member is absent. Technically, the Transit and Parking Commission makes recommendations to the Council regarding policies on all transit matters within the meaning of Section 66.943 of Wisconsin State Statutes. Issues include contracts, bus stop location, vehicle purchases, fares, schedules and routes. Subcommittees advisory to this main body include an ADA Transit Subcommittee, a Disabled Parking Subcommittee, and a Contracted Services Oversight Committee.
