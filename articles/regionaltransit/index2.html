Regional Transit: Main Page (1)

At present, there is no regional transit system in the Madison area. We need one. The closest we come to a regional system at present is that Madison's Metro Transit contracts with several neighboring areas -- the cities of Fitchburg, Middleton, and Verona -- to run special bus lines. The small City of Monona maintains its own, limited, transit service. The cities of Sun Prairie and Stoughton provide limited shared-ride taxi service. Otherwise, there is no public transit service in the region.

Unfortunately, instead of planning to expand our present transit system, transit planners wasted precious years devising a grandiose rail scheme (first light rail then heavy rail) that would have cannibalized Madison's bus system. While this can be traced to at least the 1990s, the last scheme was proposed to the Federal Transit Administration in 2008, called Transport 2020.

Alarmed by its disregard for existing transit systems and their woes, the Madison Area Bus Advocates was formed in late 2005 to advocate for expanding and improving on what already existed.

Included in a regional systemwould be a terminal for intercity bus travel. There used to be one. But it was eliminated in 2009 during the administration of then-mayor David Ciezlewicz. Although its demise was unfortunate, we are also now in the position to plan for a more modern inter-modal and multi-use facility.

Between late 2009 and early 2011 there was a short-lived beginning of a Dane County Regional Transit Authority. The governing Board dafted a preliminary Plan for Transit but, in the face of imminent elimination in early 2011 by a new state administration, never took a final plan to referendum.
