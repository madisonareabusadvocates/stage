Don't increase bus fares

The Capital Times, November 30, 2008

Dear Editor: I attended the bus hearing last Monday, and like most people oppose any fare increase.

At the hearing I was told only the commission members or the Madison Metro administrators could ask questions.

Earlier in the day I had called the Metro administration, by they did not return my call.

My questions are:

Do the receipts for bus fares and sales of advertising go directly into the Metro budget?

Do grant monies from the federal and state governments for transportation have earmarks for specific amounts allocated to Madison Metro?

As I stated at the hearing, the bus service is a public service, just like fire, police, public health, schools, etc.

Last year when the streets department ran out of budgeted funds for snowplowing, the mayor and council had no problem giving them more.

Thomas Kozlovsky, Madison
