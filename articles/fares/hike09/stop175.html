Stop at 25-cent hike in bus fare

The Capital Times, February 21, 2009

Dear Editor:

The bus fare increase from $1.50 to $1.75 approved by the Transit and Park Commission should be accepted by the council and the mayor. This is a compromise that all can live with.

Most of the increase is not going to improve the service, but for reserves, a marketing person and policing of transfer points. It is the responsible of the police to police the transfer points, just like for parks, streets, neighborhoods and bars.

The fare increase will affect all riders -- low-income, seniors or even those with passes. Employers who provide free or reduced passes will also have to pay more.

The bus is a basic service and should be funded as such. A decrease in ridership would weaken, not strengthen, it. Milwaukee is proposing a fare decrease on some of its routes.

If the mayor and city can afford a $200,000 snowmaking machine, it can afford the estimated balance of $200,00 of the Metro budget after the 25-cent fare hike.

Thomas Kozlovsky, Madison
