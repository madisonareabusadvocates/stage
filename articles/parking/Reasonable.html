Be reasonable in setting parking fines

The Wisconsin State Journal
February 3
2010

The plan by the city of Madison to raise parking fines to raise revenue is a really bad idea. This will fall on the backs of many people least able to afford another parking citation. The reality is many receive parking citations that they cannot afford to pay.

Ignoring or just leaving the citations unpaid may result in license suspension. This may lead to citations for operating with suspended registration, so now the person has to deal with not only parking issues but traffic citations and possible financial and legal problems.

Im not suggesting Madison should not have parking rules. The city needs to be able to regulate traffic and parking. But when the goal of enforcement is to raise money, the enforcement methods can become unreasonable and overly vigilant. In some cases the issuance of a parking citations is a public relations disaster.

I think parking enforcement on the UW-Madison campus has added a component of reasonableness and discretion to campus parking enforcement. The city might want to try this approach.
