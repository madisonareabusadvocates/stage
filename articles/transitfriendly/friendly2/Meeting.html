First meeting tonight on bus transfer sites

The Capital Times
September 24
1996

Madison Metro will hold the first of five public meetings on bus transfer points at 7 p.m. tonight at Midvale Lutheran Church, 4329 Tokay Blvd.

The proposed transfer points are part of a restructured route system intended to provide more flexibility for riders and to shorten transit trips to peripheral areas.

East Milwaukee Street/ Corporate Drive: This site is the former Sports Pub location running parallel to Milwaukee Street from Corporate Drive.

North — Aberg Avneue: This site is adjacent to Huxley Street south of Aberg Avenue on property owned by Oscar Mayer.

South — South Park Street: Several sites are under consideration.

Southwest — Research Park: This site is in the new Research Park addition west of Whitney Way, running parallel to Tokay Boulevard.

West — Hill Farms State Transportation Building: This site is at the western edge of the visitor lot, adjacent to the Red Cross.

The transfer points will be designed to hold from six to eight buses along a center island with bus parking along the sides. A canopy will cover the island and extend slightly over the top of the buses to protect passengers from inclement weather. Lighting and passenger information also will be provided.

Buses will arrive and depart the transfer points on a coordinated schedle for immediate transfer between buses.

The four other meetings on the transfer points will be at:

South Madison Library, 2222 S. Park, 6:30 p.m. Oct. 7. Hilldale Mall conference room, 7 p.m. Oct. 21. Pinney Library, 204 Cottage Grove Rd., 7 p.m. Oct. 23. Sherman School, 1610 Ruskin, 7 p.m. Oct. 28.

For more information, call Sharon Persich, Metro planning and scheduling manager, at 266-6545 from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through Friday.
