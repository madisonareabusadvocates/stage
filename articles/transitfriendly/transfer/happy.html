Happy with buses:

The Capital Times, April 11, 2001
From City Notebook (p.3A) by Judith Davidoff

Although both the public and city officials have recently questioned the wisdom of switching to a hub transfer system, Madison Metro riders remain generally satisfied with bus service, according to a recent survey of bus riders.

The survey, which polled riders on weekday trips, found that 81 percent of riders rated service as "good" or "very good," down slightly from a 1997 survey when 86 percent polled rated service 'as "good" or "very good."

The survey also found that most riders use the bus to commute to work and that the number of riders who have a license to drive — 75 percent — has decreased since 1985.

"This indicates that the percentage of Metro's ridership that is transit dependent is increasing," said the report's authors.
